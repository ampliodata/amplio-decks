/06 — OUTREACH CADENCE

The 4-touch cadence.

Don't wing it. Don't spam. Four calibrated touches, then respectful silence for 90 days.

Day 0

Touch 1 — First contact

Cold LinkedIn DM or email. Under 100 words. Lead with their problem, not our solution. End with a question.

Day 3–4

Touch 2 — Follow-up

Friendly, not pushy. Reference a case study or useful resource. Under 80 words.

Day 8–11

Touch 3 — New angle

Share a blog post, insight, or checklist relevant to their specific challenge. Educational, not salesy.

Day 15–21

Touch 4 — Graceful exit

Final attempt. "If things change, I'm here." Then silence for 90 days.

Rules of the road

  • → Cold: under 100 words. Warm: under 150.
  • → Always personalize — research their company, recent activity, visible challenges.
  • → Never more than one emoji. Zero is ideal.
  • → Lead with their problem. Not our solution.
  • → End with a question or soft CTA. Never a hard ask.
  • → If they respond at any touch → shift to discovery/qualification mode.
  • → No em-dashes in LinkedIn posts (they render poorly on mobile).

Remember

If they respond at ANY touch, abandon the cadence. Shift immediately to discovery mode. Your job stops being "get them to reply" and starts being "understand their pain."

/09 — YOUR DAY-TO-DAY

What a typical day and week looks like.

A rhythm that mixes pipeline hygiene, outreach, calls, and prep. Supported by AI and the vault.

A day in your life

09:00 — Morning: Pipeline review +
Open the Sales list in Attio. Filter by "Next Action Date = Today" or "Stage = Meeting / Proposal". Check which deals need follow-up. Review any new leads that came in from the scraper overnight. Pull up Gmail for any replies from yesterday's outreach.
10:30 — Outreach block +
Work through qualified leads in the Sales list. Send personalised LinkedIn DMs or emails using the outreach templates (adapted to the persona). Update outreach_status and last_contacted_date on each record. Log notes on what you said.
14:00 — Discovery calls & proposals +
Take discovery calls. After each call, update the company record in Attio: move the Sales list stage forward, add notes, update the pitch angle. If ready for a proposal, move to "Proposal" stage and note the recommended tier. Pull the proposal template from the vault.
17:00 — End of day: Update & plan +
Review everything you touched today. Make sure all Attio records are up to date. Set "Next Action" and "Next Action Date" on every active deal — no deal should ever be "stale". Flag any deals that need Walter or Daragh's input. Drop notes in memory.md if anything needs to carry over.

A week in your life

MONDAY

Pipeline review & planning

Review all active deals in Attio. Check which prospects need follow-up. Plan the week's outreach targets. Review new scraped leads.

TUE · WED · THU

Core selling days

Send personalised outreach (LinkedIn DM + email). Run discovery calls. Send proposals. Update Attio after every touchpoint.

FRIDAY

Reporting & prep

Update pipeline numbers. Flag deals that need Walter or Daragh's help. Prep for next week's calls. Review vault for cross-client patterns.

Target rhythm

Aim for 15–25 fresh outreach touches per day. Every deal in the Sales list should have a "Next Action" and a date — always.

/10 — BRAND VOICE

Sound like a smart, experienced colleague.

Every message represents Amplio. Here's how we talk — and how we don't.

DO THIS

On-brand examples

Confident, not arrogant:
"We've lifted ROAS by 23% on average."

Direct, not blunt:
"Your GA4 isn't tracking purchases correctly — here's why."

Technical, not intimidating:
"Server-side GTM isolates conversion data from third-party cookies."

Warm, not casual:
"Let's fix this tracking gap together."

NOT THIS

Off-brand examples

Arrogant:
"We're the best data experts you'll find."

Blunt:
"Your setup is broken and amateur."

Intimidating:
"You need sGTM implementation asap."

Too casual:
"Yo, ur tracking is sus lol."

Preferred terms

Say tracking — not instrumentation.
Say insights / intelligence — not learnings.
Say setup / implementation — not deployment.
Say fix / solve — not optimize / enhance.
Say AI-powered — not ML / algorithm-driven.
Say privacy-compliant — not GDPR-ready.

Style rules

Length: 15–20 words max per sentence.
Voice: Active, not passive.
Pronouns: Use "you" and "your" liberally.
Numbers: Specific, never vague.
Verbs: fix, solve, prove, embed, automate — not leverage, synergize.
Proof points: Only verified ones. Never fabricate.
