Qualification

Red Flags — Disqualify If:

  • No website — Can't track what doesn't exist
  • In-house analytics team 5+ — They'll build their own
  • Enterprise 1000+ — Need dedicated account team (not our model)
  • No digital marketing activity — No tracking need
  • Direct competitor — Risk + conflict of interest
  • Non-commercial entity — Different business model
  • Financial distress signals — Late payments, layoffs, losing clients

Remember: Disqualify early. It's better to say "not a fit" than spend 3 months with a client who can't succeed.

Sales

Outreach Framework

Cold Outreach Formula

Under 100 words: Hook → Problem → Proof → CTA (soft)

Example: "Hey [Name], saw you just added GTM to [company]. Most teams leave 20% of conversions untracked. We fixed it for [similar company] in 48h. Worth a quick call?"

Warm Follow-up

Under 150 words: Personal context → Specific insight → Value → CTA

Example: "Following up from my message. I noticed [company] is running multi-channel campaigns. Most teams we talk to have 15–30% tracking gaps across channels. We've built a quick audit tool—happy to run it for you, no strings."

Discovery Questions by Persona

  • Agencies: "How much of your project timeline goes to tracking setup?"
  • Performance: "Are your attribution numbers consistent across GA4, Meta, and actual revenue?"
  • CMO: "How confident are you in your marketing ROI numbers right now?"
  • E-commerce: "Do you trust your purchase tracking in GA4?"
  • Startup: "What's your current funnel visibility?"

Best Channels by Persona

  • Agency Owner: LinkedIn DM (personal, discreet)
  • Performance Lead: Email (direct to inbox, professional)
  • CMO: Warm intro (builds credibility)
  • E-commerce Manager: Email (less intrusive than DM)
  • Founder: LinkedIn / Twitter (where they hang out)

Communication

Brand Voice Quick Reference

How We Sound

Like a smart, experienced colleague who genuinely wants to help.

  • Confident, not arrogant
  • Direct, not blunt
  • Technical, not intimidating
  • Warm, not casual

Power Verbs (Use These)

  • fix — implies urgency + expertise
  • solve — problem-focused
  • prove — credibility-focused
  • embed — long-term partnership
  • automate — saves time
  • audit — independent, rigorous

Words to Avoid

❌ optimize · enhance · deploy · leverage · synergize · solution · empower · paradigm shift

Specificity Rule

Specific numbers beat vague qualifiers.

✓ "+23% ROAS lift" vs. ✗ "significant improvement"
✓ "48h setup" vs. ✗ "quick deployment"
✓ "€2M–€30M revenue" vs. ✗ "mid-market"
