The AI-powered prospecting engine

We don't just cold-call random companies. We have an automated pipeline that scrapes websites, scores them based on their tracking setup, and identifies exactly who needs us and why.

Step 1

Website Scraping

Our AI scraper crawls prospect websites and extracts technographic signals: GTM present? GA4 client-side or server-side? CMP missing or misconfigured? Ad platforms running?

Step 2

ICP Scoring

Each prospect gets a composite score (0-100) based on: Industry (25%), Size (15%), Tech signals (20%), Ad spend (15%), Trigger events (15%), Contact quality (5%), Geography (5%).

Step 3

Opportunity Classification

HIGH: Missing CMP/tracking — immediate need. MID: Basics present, gaps exist. LOW: Sophisticated setup — partnership angle.

Outreach cadence

Touch 1 — Day 0

Cold LinkedIn DM or email. Under 100 words. Lead with their problem, not our solution. End with a question.

Touch 2 — Day 3-4

Follow-up DM or email. Friendly, not pushy. Reference a case study or useful resource.

Touch 3 — Day 8-11

New angle. Share a blog post, insight, or checklist relevant to their specific challenge.

Touch 4 — Day 15-21

Final attempt. Graceful exit. "If things change, I'm here." Then silence for 90 days.

/ATTIO CRM

How we use Attio

Attio is our CRM. Everything flows through it — from scraped leads to active clients. Here's how it's structured and how you'll use it daily.

Core objects

Companies

The anchor of everything. Matched by domain (unique key). Contains firmographic data, ICP score, technographic signals, and outreach status.

Key fields you'll use: ICP (Hot/Warm/Cool), Lead Score (0-100), Opportunity Tier (HIGH/MID/LOW), Outreach Status, Pitch Angle.

People

Contacts linked to companies. Matched by email (unique key). Has persona type, job title, decision authority, and preferred contact channel.

Key fields you'll use: Persona Type, Decision Authority, Preferred Channel, Job Title.

Deals

Revenue opportunities linked to companies + people. Tracks deal value (EUR), service tier, and pipeline stage.

Key fields you'll use: Deal Stage, Deal Value, Service Tier, Deal Type.

Lists (your daily views)

Sales List

Primary

Track every company from first touch to close. This is your main working view.

Lead → Qualified → Meeting → Proposal → Won

Key entry fields: Stage, Priority (High/Medium/Low), Estimated Contract Value (EUR), Projected Close Date, Next Action.

Client Success List

Post-sale

Track active clients through their lifecycle. Updated by account management.

Onboarding → Steady → Expansion → Renewal

Key entry fields: Health (Excellent/Good/Fair/Risk), MRR, Services Active, Next Review Date.

Data flow: how leads get into Attio

1. SCRAPER

AI crawls websites, extracts GTM/GA4/CMP signals, scores with ICP matrix, classifies opportunity tier.

2. CRM ENRICHMENT

Upserts company record (matched by domain), creates notes with signal breakdown, adds to Sales list as "Lead".

3. OUTREACH

Finds contacts, creates People records, writes personalised messages, updates outreach status.

Your daily Attio workflow

Morning: Review pipeline ▶
Open the Sales list in Attio. Filter by "Next Action Date = Today" or "Stage = Meeting / Proposal". Check which deals need follow-up. Review any new leads that came in from scraping overnight.
Mid-morning: Outreach block ▶
Work through qualified leads in the Sales list. Send personalised LinkedIn DMs or emails using the outreach templates (adapted to the persona). Update outreach_status and last_contacted_date on each record. Log notes.
Afternoon: Discovery calls & proposals ▶
Take discovery calls. After each call, update the company record in Attio: move the Sales list stage forward, add notes, update the pitch angle. If ready for proposal, move to "Proposal" stage and note the recommended tier.
End of day: Update & plan ▶
Review everything you touched today. Make sure all Attio records are up to date. Set "Next Action" and "Next Action Date" on all active deals. Flag any deals that need Walter's input.

/QUICK REFERENCE

Brand voice cheat sheet

Every message you send represents Amplio. Here's the voice in a nutshell.

Do this

Confident, not arrogant: "We've lifted ROAS by 23% on average."
Direct, not blunt: "Your GA4 isn't tracking purchases correctly — here's why."
Technical, not intimidating: "Server-side GTM isolates conversion data from third-party cookies."
Warm, not casual: "Let's fix this tracking gap together."

Not this

Arrogant: "We're the best data experts you'll find."
Blunt: "Your setup is broken and amateur."
Intimidating: "You need sGTM implementation asap."
Too casual: "Yo, ur tracking is sus lol."

Preferred terms

Say tracking (not instrumentation).
Say insights (not learnings).
Say setup (not deployment).
Say fix / solve (not optimize / enhance).
Say AI-powered (not ML / algorithm-driven).

Email rules

Cold: Under 100 words.
Warm: Under 150 words.
Structure: Hook → Problem → Proof → CTA (soft).
Tone: Respectful, curious, specific to their situation.
End with: A question, not a hard ask.
